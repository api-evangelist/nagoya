Nagoya University researchers developed a silver nanoparticle technology that achieves 2 to 5 times higher DNA assembly efficiency than conventional restriction enzyme methods, enabling more precise cutting and joining of DNA. Applications include advanced crop breeding, genetic disease treatment, and generating animal models for drug discovery.
